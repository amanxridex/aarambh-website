About the Project

Hanumat Vihar Awasiya Yojna is a RERA-approved, MVDA-sanctioned residential plotted township on Radha Kund – Ral Road, Vrindavan — one of India’s most revered spiritual destinations.

Designed for families, investors, retirees and those seeking a second home, the township blends the calm of Vrindavan’s spiritual landscape with modern infrastructure. With clear legal titles, structured planning and a rapidly appreciating corridor, Hanumat Vihar offers a rare opportunity to own land at the heart of Braj Bhoomi.

“Owning land in Vrindavan is not just a financial decision — it is a meaningful life investment.”

The project suits buyers who wish to build a house, retire in peace, create a holiday home or simply secure a long-term family asset in a location where land values continue to rise year after year.

Infrastructure & Amenities

Every aspect planned with precision — from CBR-based road design to an integrated sewage treatment plant.

01

Road Network & Footpaths

Main arterial roads with flexible pavements designed to CBR soil values. Sub-arteries use flexible or rigid pavements. All footpaths finished with water-permeable material for rainwater percolation.

02

Water Supply

Underground water drawn until UP Jal Nigam supply is available. Per capita consumption designed at 135 lpcd as per Ministry of Housing & Urban Development guidelines.

03

Sewer System & STP

Sub-mains in front of all plots connected to trunk mains, terminating at a Sewage Treatment Plant. Rainwater harvesting pits at multiple locations with overflow to external drainage.

04

Boundary Wall & Plot Demarcation

Developer constructs perimeter boundary wall around the entire society. Individual plot demarcations done on-site per approved MVDA layout. Multiple plot sizes available.

05

Parks & Landscaping

All parks developed with tot-lot areas, jogging tracks, shrubs and shady trees. Diverse tree species planted along road peripheries and site boundaries.

06

Electrical Supply & Street Lighting

Network designed as per Indian Electricity Act, UP Electricity Supply Code 2005 and UPERC regulations for township distribution networks.

07

Solid Waste Management

Door-to-door waste collection via a dedicated maintenance agency. Garbage bins and collection points distributed throughout the township.

08

Community Buildings

Community infrastructure developed as per the approved layout plan, providing shared spaces for all residents.
